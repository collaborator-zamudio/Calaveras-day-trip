Bottom line: All three spots sit within a 25–40 minute loop of 326 Sunrise Rd and of each other — no early 5:30 AM departure needed this time. Best single-day combo for this group: Columbia State Historic Park (morning) + Moaning Caverns Spiral Tour (early afternoon). Mercer Caverns is a strong alternative to Moaning Caverns but has no online booking and a tougher staircase (208 down / 232 up) — Moaning Caverns' tour is shorter, bookable online, and the deepest cave in California. Doing all three in one day is possible but tight with 4 young kids; recommend picking two.

02 Columbia State Historic Park
Best fit for the group

Columbia State Historic Park

22708 Main St, Columbia, CA — ~35–40 min from 326 Sunrise Rd
Free entry No stairs Self-paced Stroller-friendly

A preserved 1850s Gold Rush town — the best-preserved Gold Rush town in California. Wooden sidewalks, historic shops, a working blacksmith, ice cream parlor, and two hotels. Park exhibits open daily 10 a.m.–5 p.m. May 1 through September 30, so the whole park will be open July 18.

Top activities for the kids:

• Gold panning at Matelot Gulch Mining Co. — staff teach panning technique in a flume; kids keep what they find. Roughly $10/person, no reservation needed, open daily.
• Stagecoach ride — the Quartz Mountain Stage Line operates horse-drawn rides around town, roughly $15 per person, seasonal April–October — running in July.
• Free guided walking tour — departs from the museum at 11 a.m. daily mid-June through Labor Day, a relaxed 45–60 min overview.
• Candle-dipping, period dress-up at the museum, Fallon Ice Cream Parlor.

Cost: Free entry · ~$10/person gold panning · ~$15/person stagecoach (both optional)
⚠ Summer temperatures in the Sierra foothills run hot — dirt streets, little shade midday. Aim for a 9–11 AM arrival.

Hours confirmed for July 18: Summer hours run 7 days/week through August 17, 2026, 10 AM–4 PM, last tour departs 4 PM. Saturday is fully open — no closure risk.
Good for the group

Spiral Tour

5350 Moaning Cave Rd, Vallecito, CA — ~25–30 min from 326 Sunrise Rd
45 min 235 steps Ages 5+ Leaves hourly

The Spiral Tour descends a 144-step spiral staircase (built in 1922 from scrap WWI battleship metal) into the largest single cave chamber in California — big enough to fit the Statue of Liberty. Guided, 45 minutes, leaves every hour on the hour. Wheelchairs and strollers cannot navigate the stairs, but active 5–8 year-olds handle it fine with an adult alongside.

Ages 3–11: $20.14 · Ages 12+: $27.56 · Under 3: Free (prices include online booking fee)
⚠ Backpacks, bags, water bottles, and food are not allowed inside the cave — leave them in the car or with a designated adult who isn't touring.

04 Mercer Caverns

Standard Cave Tour

1665 Sheep Ranch Rd, Murphys, CA — ~30–35 min from 326 Sunrise Rd
~1 hr 440 total steps 161 ft deep

One of California's most decorated show caves — over 125 years of tours. Visitors descend 208 stairs and climb back up 232 (440 total) through chambers with stalactites, stalagmites, flowstone, and a rare aragonite formation. Reviewers note the tour is more horizontal with ups and downs, compared to Moaning Caverns' single vertical descent.

Effective Jan 1, 2026: Adults $24.00 · Children ages 3–12: $18.00 · Group rates available for 10+
⚠ No online ticket purchase — walk-in only, pay at the counter. With an 11-person group on a Saturday in July, arrive early to avoid a wait for the next tour slot.

06 Tips for This Group
🌡️

Beat the heat at Columbia

Sierra foothill summers run hot with little shade on the dirt streets. Arrive by 9:30–10 AM and plan the cave tour (naturally 61°F underground) for the hottest part of the afternoon.

🧥

Bring a layer for the caves

Both Moaning Caverns and Mercer Caverns stay a constant 61°F year-round. Light jackets or sweatshirts for kids and adults, even on a hot July day.

👟

Footwear for stairs

Closed-toe shoes with grip for both caverns — Moaning Caverns has 235 steps, Mercer has 440. Sandals are not appropriate on either tour.

🎒

No bags inside Moaning Caverns

Backpacks, purses, water bottles, food, and drinks are not allowed inside the Moaning Caverns tour for safety reasons. Designate one adult to stay topside with everyone's belongings, or leave them in the car.

🚫

Strollers won't work underground

Neither cave tour accommodates strollers or wheelchairs due to stairs. If any child needs to be carried, plan for an adult to carry them through — front carriers are fine at Moaning Caverns, baby backpacks are not.

💵

Bring cash and cards

Mercer Caverns has no online ticket sales — pay in person. Columbia's gold panning and stagecoach vendors may prefer cash. Bring both options.

🚧

Gated community exit

326 Sunrise Rd sits inside the Lake Tulloch Shores gated community, accessed via Hwy 4 to O'Byrnes Ferry Rd. Build a couple extra minutes into departure for both cars to clear the gate together.

🅿️

Two-car coordination

All three sites have free, ample parking — far less pressure than Yosemite. Still worth agreeing on a single meeting point at each stop given the group size.

🍦

Build in a treat stop

Fallon Ice Cream Parlor in Columbia is a built-in reward after the walking and gold panning — good incentive for kids mid-morning or after lunch.
